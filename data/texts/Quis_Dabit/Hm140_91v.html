Folio 91 Verso
Compare Witnesses: •
To all folkis that stondis in repentaunce •
With hert contryte made their confessioun •
Of will and thought accomplisshid þe penaunce •
And there doone satisfaccioun •
That clayme by meane of crystis passioun •
markid with tave for more surte •
To theym Ihesu shałł graunte fułł pardon •
To aske hym mercy whan they knelle on her kne. •
In this name most souernyne of vertue •
Stondeth hole oure hope and all our assureance •
ffor when that euer named is Ihesu •
Ageyne goostly trouble menn bere allegeaunce •
Who trustith jhesu may fele no grevaunce •
Which from ałł thraldom brought vs to liberte •
Oute of all servage he made oon acquytaunce •
To all that knele to Ihesu on ther kne •
In amerous hartis brennyng of kyndenesse •
This name of Ihesu moost profundly doth myne •
Martir Ignacius can bere þerof witnesse •
A myd whos hert by grace which is diviene •
With auriet letters as golde that did schyne •
His hert was grave men may this legand se •
To teche all christen there hedis to enclyne •
To blessed Ihesu and bow adown here kne •
By bolde Ihesu made ower redempcioun1 •
With water of Baptym from fylth wessh vs clene •
And from his hert two licours ther ran downe •
On caluery, the truthe was wele sene •
Whan longyns with a spere can •
percyed his hert a pon the rode tree •
O man kynde thynke what this doth mene •
And vnto hym bowe a downe thy knee •
Notes
  1. This stanza appears as number six here, but is number seven in the order provided by MacCracken in EETS 107. ↩
